How Blood Donation Works

The Journey of Your Donation

When you donate blood, your contribution goes through several important steps before reaching a patient in need:

  1. Registration & Screening - Your vital signs are checked and eligibility confirmed
  2. Donation - The actual donation takes only 8-10 minutes
  3. Processing & Testing - Your donation is tested for blood type and infectious diseases
  4. Storage - Blood components are stored at specific temperatures
  5. Distribution - Your blood is sent to hospitals where it's needed most
  6. Transfusion - Your donation saves lives by reaching patients in emergency situations

Blood Donation Eligibility

General Requirements

  • Age between 18-65 years
  • Weight above 45 kg
  • Hemoglobin count above 12.5 g/dl
  • Normal blood pressure
  • Last donation was more than 3 months ago

You Can Donate If You

  • Are in good health
  • Had a good night's sleep
  • Had a meal within last 4 hours
  • Have not had any recent surgeries
  • Have no active medical conditions

You Cannot Donate If You

  • Have a cold, flu, or fever
  • Are pregnant or recently gave birth
  • Have certain medical conditions
  • Had recent major surgery
  • Are taking certain medications

Frequently Asked Questions

Am I Eligible to Donate Blood?

General requirements include being in good health, at least 18 years old (16-17 with parental consent in some areas), weighing at least 110 pounds, and not having donated in the last 56 days. Certain medical conditions, medications, travel history, and recent tattoos or piercings may affect eligibility. Our pre-screening form will help determine your eligibility.

How Often Can I Donate Blood?

Whole blood donors can donate every 56 days (about 8 weeks). Platelet donors can donate every 7 days up to 24 times per year. Plasma donors can donate every 28 days. Double red cell donors can donate every 112 days (about 16 weeks).

How Long Does the Donation Process Take?

The entire process takes about one hour from the time you arrive until the time you leave. The actual blood donation only takes about 8-10 minutes. The rest of the time is spent on registration, health history review, and a brief physical examination.

What Should I Do Before and After Donating?

Before donating: Eat a healthy meal, drink plenty of water, and get adequate rest. After donating: Drink extra fluids, avoid strenuous physical activity for 24 hours, and keep the bandage on for several hours. If you feel dizzy, lie down with your feet elevated until the feeling passes.

How Do I Request Blood in an Emergency?

For emergencies, use our "Urgent Blood Request" feature. Enter the patient's location, blood type, and quantity needed. Our system will immediately notify compatible donors in the vicinity and connect you with nearby blood banks that have availability. Medical professionals can also initiate requests through our verified healthcare provider portal.
